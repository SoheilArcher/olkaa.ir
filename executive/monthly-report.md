# Monthly Executive Report

Version: v1.0.0  
Status: Report architecture

The Monthly Executive Report gives leadership a financial, operational, and strategic view of the company month.

## Purpose

- Review monthly revenue, MRR, ARR, cash flow, and bank balance.
- Review product and project performance.
- Track customer growth and support quality.
- Review roadmap execution and technical debt.
- Capture monthly decisions, ADRs, lessons, and risks.

## Report Sections

1. Monthly Executive Summary
2. Revenue
3. Monthly Revenue
4. MRR
5. ARR
6. Cash Flow
7. Bank Balance
8. Product Portfolio Review
9. Project Delivery Review
10. Customer Review
11. Support Performance
12. Infrastructure Uptime and Incidents
13. Mail, VPN, and Server Health
14. Security Review
15. AI Usage Review
16. Roadmap Progress
17. Documentation Coverage
18. Technical Debt
19. Research Progress
20. Upcoming Releases
21. Recent Decisions
22. Recent ADRs
23. Open Risks
24. Next Month Priorities

## Finance Requirements

Monthly finance summaries should separate:

- Recognized revenue.
- Recurring revenue.
- One-time revenue.
- Outstanding invoices.
- Cash received.
- Cash paid.
- Bank balance.
- Payroll obligations.
- Vendor obligations.
- Financial risks.

## Output

The monthly report should become the official leadership review artifact for that month.

## Approval

Suggested approval chain:

1. Finance validates financial data.
2. Module owners validate operational data.
3. CEO approves the monthly report.
4. Approved report is archived.
