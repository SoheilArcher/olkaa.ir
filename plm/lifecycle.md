# Product Lifecycle

Version: v1.0.0  
Status: Initial system

## Idea

An initial product or feature concept.

Required evidence:

- Problem statement.
- Proposed value.
- Potential users or customers.
- Initial owner.

## Research

The concept is being investigated.

Required evidence:

- Research question.
- Sources or experiments.
- Risks and assumptions.
- Recommendation.

## Prototype

A limited proof of concept is being explored.

Required evidence:

- Prototype scope.
- Success criteria.
- Evaluation notes.
- Known limitations.

## MVP

A minimum viable product is defined for validation.

Required evidence:

- MVP scope.
- Excluded scope.
- Acceptance criteria.
- Target users.
- Security and documentation requirements.

## Development

The product is actively being built.

Required evidence:

- Approved roadmap.
- Feature registry.
- Technical design.
- Test strategy.
- Product owner.

## Testing

The product is being verified before production use.

Required evidence:

- Test plan.
- Bug list.
- Release checklist.
- Security review.
- Documentation review.

## Production

The product is delivered, verified, supported, and ready for operational use.

Required evidence:

- Release record.
- Support owner.
- Monitoring or operational plan.
- Security controls.
- User documentation.

## Maintenance

The product is stable and receiving fixes, updates, support, and small improvements.

Required evidence:

- Maintenance owner.
- Bug policy.
- Release process.
- Technical debt review.

## Deprecated

The product is no longer recommended for new use.

Required evidence:

- Deprecation reason.
- Replacement or migration plan.
- Communication plan.
- Support timeline.

## Archived

The product is retired and preserved for historical reference.

Required evidence:

- Archive date.
- Final version.
- Historical documentation.
- Data retention decision.
