# Fava Imen Olka Enterprise Roadmap

## Company Vision

Fava Imen Olka aims to become a disciplined enterprise technology company that combines documented operational experience, reliable engineering standards, airport and infrastructure expertise, and carefully governed AI-enabled products into a coherent ecosystem managed through a single source of truth.

# Strategic Timeline

## 2026

- Establish the Enterprise Manual as the official company source of truth.
- Define the Enterprise Operating System as the executive management layer.
- Complete baseline product specifications, roadmaps, engineering standards, GitHub governance, website content architecture, and knowledge management structure.
- Define MVP scope for Airport ERP.
- Keep Market AI in research until data governance and evaluation standards are approved.

## 2027

- Validate Airport ERP MVP workflows through documented internal review or approved pilot work.
- Create detailed technical specifications for VPN Accounting, Mail Server, NetLog, URL Reporting, Active Directory, and AI Call Center.
- Establish recurring executive review cadence using the Enterprise Operating System.
- Improve documentation coverage across products, architecture, security, and operations.

## 2028

- Move validated product lines from planning into controlled development when ownership, requirements, and security standards are ready.
- Mature deployment, support, monitoring, backup, and release processes.
- Expand Enterprise Design System adoption across product interfaces.
- Introduce AI-assisted workflows only where evaluation and governance are documented.

## 2029

- Develop a more unified ecosystem across airport operations, enterprise systems, infrastructure services, identity, reporting, and AI workflows.
- Convert verified project experience into approved case studies and executive reports.
- Formalize a research-to-product pipeline for AI, security, networking, automation, and airport technology.

## 2030

- Maintain a portfolio of mature, documented, and reviewable enterprise products.
- Use the Enterprise Operating System for company KPIs, risks, roadmap progress, executive reports, and strategic decisions.
- Continue preserving company memory through decisions, ADRs, lessons learned, research, innovation records, and the engineering knowledge base.

# Risks

## Business

- Product scope may expand faster than company capacity.
- Client references may be used publicly before approval.
- Revenue and customer metrics may be incomplete until the Enterprise Operating System is populated.

## Technical

- Product architecture may become inconsistent without ADRs and standards enforcement.
- Integrations may be more complex than expected.
- Technical debt may remain invisible without regular executive review.

## Operational

- Roadmaps may become outdated if review cadence is not followed.
- Support, monitoring, backup, and release processes may lag behind product planning.
- Documentation may fragment if new decisions are not added to the manual.

## Security

- AI, customer, network, and operational data require strict access control.
- Products must define audit logs, data protection, and secure deployment before production use.
- Secrets, credentials, and private data must never be committed.

# Executive Dashboard

Executive metrics should include:

- Revenue: monthly revenue, recurring revenue, outstanding invoices, and revenue risks.
- Product portfolio: status, health, owner, roadmap progress, and next milestone for each product.
- Project delivery: active projects, blocked items, overdue milestones, and next actions.
- Customers: active customers, relationship status, open risks, and account ownership.
- Contracts: contract status, renewal risks, obligations, and financial indicators.
- Employees: team capacity, responsibilities, key skills, and workload risks.
- Roadmap progress: company and product roadmap completion by period.
- Technical debt: product, architecture, infrastructure, security, and documentation debt.
- Documentation coverage: product specs, standards, ADRs, decisions, lessons, and knowledge-base updates.
- AI usage: approved use cases, evaluation status, data sensitivity, and risk notes.
- Infrastructure status: server, network, backup, monitoring, and incident indicators.
- Security status: access reviews, audit-log coverage, vulnerabilities, and incident follow-up.
