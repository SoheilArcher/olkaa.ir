# Documentation Standards

Version: v1.0.0  
Status: Initial standard

## Documentation First

- Major products, features, workflows, and strategic decisions should be documented before or during implementation.
- Documentation is part of delivery, not an afterthought.
- The Enterprise Manual is the source of truth for company knowledge.

## Required Metadata

Where applicable, documents should include:

- Version.
- Status.
- Date.
- Author.
- Related product.
- Related technologies.

## Style

- Use professional English.
- Use clean Markdown.
- Do not overclaim.
- Separate completed, in-progress, research, and planned work.
- Prefer updating existing documents over creating duplicates.

## Historical Knowledge

- Never delete historical knowledge without a replacement or superseding note.
- Use dated updates when facts change.
- Record lessons learned from incidents, deployments, and project outcomes.

## Links

- Link product documents to roadmaps, decisions, ADRs, standards, and knowledge-base entries where useful.
- Keep public-facing content aligned with approved company and product documentation.
